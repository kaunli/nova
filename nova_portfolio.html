The Problem

E-commerce is broken.
Nobody's admitting it.

Amazon and its competitors have quietly turned shopping into a minefield of manipulative patterns, hidden incentives, and information asymmetry. Buyers lose. Every time.

🎭
Ads disguised as results
Sponsored products are indistinguishable from organic ones. You think you're seeing the best option. You're seeing the highest bidder.
📉
Fake discounts
"60% off" means nothing without price history. Platforms routinely inflate "was" prices to manufacture urgency that isn't real.
🤖
2,000 reviews, zero clarity
Thousands of reviews — many fake, many paid — with no synthesis. Buyers are left to wade through noise without a verdict.
The Solution

Six features that change
everything about shopping.

Nova isn't a better Amazon. It's a fundamentally different premise — where every design decision is made in the buyer's interest, not the advertiser's.

👁️
Transparent recommendations
Every product card shows exactly why it appeared — your browsing behaviour, local trends, or an editorial pick. Zero paid placements, ever. Users always know what they're looking at and why.
TRUST · HONESTY
🤖
AI review summaries
Instead of wading through thousands of reviews — many fake, many paid — Nova's AI distils them into clear pros, cons, and a plain-English verdict per product, in seconds. No guesswork.
AI · CLARITY
📈
6-month price history
A full price chart is built into every listing so you instantly see if a "deal" is real. Nova also flags products with high return rates before you commit — information Amazon buries.
PRICE INTEL · TRANSPARENCY
⚖️
Side-by-side comparison
Compare any two products with specs, ratings, and an AI-generated verdict tailored specifically to those items. No more opening 12 tabs. The comparison updates dynamically for every product you view.
DECISION SUPPORT · AI
🏅
Seller Trust Scores
Every seller is rated 1–10 based on delivery reliability, return rate, and review authenticity — not ad spend. Counterfeit sellers can't buy their way to the top. Buyers can see exactly who they're buying from.
ACCOUNTABILITY · SAFETY
⚡
2-step checkout, always
No dark patterns, no upsell popups, no last-minute fee surprises. Tax shown upfront. Deliver → Pay → Done. Checkout is treated as a service, not a revenue maximisation opportunity.
UX · ETHICS
The Pitch

Why Nova wins.
The case in plain terms.

The shopping app market is dominated by incumbents who optimise for ad revenue over user outcomes. This creates a structural gap — and a real opportunity.

Consumers are increasingly aware they're being manipulated. Search "Amazon fake reviews" and you'll find millions of results. Trust is eroding. The incumbent response has been cosmetic. A "Sponsored" label in light grey. A review filter buried in settings. Not enough.

Nova's thesis is simple: if you build genuinely for the buyer, the buyer will come. Transparency isn't just an ethical choice — it's a competitive moat. When users know Nova won't manipulate them, every recommendation carries credibility that money can't buy on competing platforms.

The business model doesn't depend on ads. It depends on trust converting to transactions — a sustainable flywheel where happy, informed buyers return more often, spend more confidently, and refer others organically.

01
The market is ready Consumer trust in e-commerce recommendations is at an all-time low. Nova enters a market that's actively looking for an alternative.
02
AI as a differentiator, not a gimmick Review synthesis, comparison verdicts, and price intelligence are AI features that directly improve purchase decisions — not chatbots for the sake of it.
03
Ethics is the product Zero ads, zero dark patterns, zero fake deals. This isn't a limitation — it's the positioning. Transparency as a brand promise, not a feature toggle.
04
Scalable trust signals Seller Trust Scores, return rate warnings, and price histories are compounding assets. The more data Nova accumulates, the more valuable and defensible the platform becomes.
